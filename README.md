## 🛠️ Performance Optimization

To ensure your projects render quickly and playback stays smooth, adjust your settings:

- Disk Cache: Go to Preferences > Media & Disk Cache. Set a dedicated folder on a fast SSD to store cache files.
- Memory: Under Preferences > Memory & Performance, allocate as much RAM as possible to After Effects.
- GPU Acceleration: Open Project Settings and ensure that Mercury GPU Acceleration (CUDA or Metal) is active. This allows your graphics card to handle the heavy lifting for effects and rendering.

## 📖 Using Key Features

After Effects uses a timeline-based system. You add layers to the timeline, then apply effects to those layers.

- Keyframes: Use keyframes to change properties like position, scale, and opacity over time.
- Effects Panel: Search for presets like "Gaussian Blur" or "Color Correction" in the Effects & Presets panel. Drag these items directly onto your footage layers.
- Composition: Your composition acts as the container for your video. Ensure your composition settings match the frame rate and resolution of your source footage.
- Exporting: When finished, select File > Export > Add to Render Queue. Choose your settings and select "Render" to create your finished video file.
